Project 01 · Supply Chain Management

Supply Chain Structure and Management

Based On: Chapter — Understanding the Supply Chain | The Handbook of Logistics and Distribution Management

Concept

Supply chain coordination across global networks

Evidence

Class notes, supply chain mapping exercise, textbook examples, and case study discussions

Skills

Process mapping, operational planning, supply chain analysis

Overview

This project focused on understanding the complete supply chain process from suppliers to manufacturers, distributors, retailers, and final customers. I learned how materials, information, and financial resources move throughout the supply chain. Effective coordination among supply chain partners helps organizations improve efficiency and customer satisfaction.

Why This Matters in Real Business

A strong supply chain reduces operational costs, improves delivery performance, and creates competitive advantages in global markets.

What Did I Learn?

  • Knowledge: I learned how different organizations collaborate within a global supply chain network.
  • Skills: Supply chain analysis, process mapping, and operational planning.
  • Reflection: Understanding how multiple organizations coordinate across countries was challenging. This concept applies directly to companies such as Amazon, Samsung, and DHL.

Project 02 · Transportation & Distribution

Transportation Mode Selection

Based On: Chapter — Transport Operations | The Handbook of Logistics and Distribution Management

Concept

Selecting transportation methods based on cost, speed, reliability, and product requirements

Evidence

Transportation comparison tables, case study findings, and logistics cost analysis

Skills

Cost analysis, logistics planning, and decision-making

Overview

This project examined various transportation modes including road, rail, sea, and air transportation. I analyzed how businesses select transportation methods based on cost, speed, reliability, and product requirements.

Transportation decisions directly affect logistics costs, customer satisfaction, and business performance.

What Did I Learn?

  • Knowledge: I learned the advantages and disadvantages of different transportation modes.
  • Skills: Cost analysis, logistics planning, and decision-making.
  • Reflection: Balancing transportation cost and delivery speed can be difficult. Companies must make these decisions every day to remain competitive.

Project 03 · Warehouse Management

Warehouse Operations and Storage Systems

Concept

Warehouse operations and their role within logistics systems

Evidence

Overview

Project 04 · Inventory Control

Inventory Control and Stock Management

Based On: Chapter — Inventory Planning and Control | The Handbook of Logistics and Distribution Management

Concept

Maintaining the right stock levels to meet demand while minimizing holding costs

Evidence

Inventory calculations, stock control examples, and textbook exercises

Skills

Inventory analysis, forecasting, and decision-making

Overview

Inventory management focuses on maintaining the right amount of stock to meet customer demand while minimizing holding costs. This project explored inventory turnover, stock control techniques, and demand forecasting methods.

Why This Matters in Real Business

Proper inventory management helps businesses avoid stock shortages and excess inventory costs.

What Did I Learn?

  • Knowledge: I learned how inventory decisions impact operational efficiency and profitability.
  • Skills: Inventory analysis, forecasting, and decision-making.
  • Reflection: Predicting future demand accurately can be difficult. Inventory management is essential for retailers, manufacturers, and e-commerce companies.
