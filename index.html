Project 01 · Network Design & Cost Analysis

Strategic Depot Consolidation Analysis

Based On: Spreadsheet Assignment — Strategic Depot Consolidation Model

Concept

Logistics network redesign using the Square Root Law of Inventory

Evidence

Spreadsheet calculations, logistics cost comparison tables, inventory reduction analysis, net savings of $770,000

Skills

Spreadsheet modeling, quantitative analysis, logistics decision-making

Overview

This project examined the impact of consolidating a logistics network from 16 small depots into 4 regional hubs. Using logistics cost calculations and the Square Root Law of Inventory, I compared storage costs, inventory levels, transportation expenses, delivery costs, and total logistics costs before and after consolidation. The goal was to determine whether the proposed network redesign would improve operational performance.

Why This Matters in Real Business

Companies frequently redesign distribution networks to improve efficiency and reduce costs. Logistics managers must evaluate trade-offs between inventory savings and transportation expenses before making strategic decisions.

What Did I Learn?

  • Knowledge: I learned how network consolidation affects inventory levels and logistics costs.
  • Skills: Spreadsheet modeling, quantitative analysis, and logistics decision-making.
  • Reflection: The most challenging aspect was balancing inventory savings against increased local delivery costs. This project demonstrated how data-driven analysis supports strategic logistics decisions.

Project 02 · Systems Thinking & Organizational Analysis

The Galactic Empire as a Logistics and Management System

Based On: Course Assignment — May the Fourth Be With You!

Concept

Systems thinking applied to complex organizational and logistics operations

Evidence

Movie analysis report, systems mapping exercise, identification of logistics and communication networks, operational challenges discussion

Skills

Critical thinking, systems analysis, and organizational evaluation

Overview

This project applied systems thinking to the Star Wars universe by analyzing the hidden logistics, transportation, communication, labor, and supply chain systems that allow the Galactic Empire to operate. By examining the Battle of Hoth in The Empire Strikes Back, I explored how multiple interconnected systems work together to support large-scale operations.

Why This Matters in Real Business

Organizations rely on interconnected systems to coordinate resources, information, and operations. Understanding these relationships helps managers improve efficiency and solve complex problems.

What Did I Learn?

  • Knowledge: I learned how systems thinking can be used to analyze complex organizations.
  • Skills: Critical thinking, systems analysis, and organizational evaluation.
  • Reflection: The most difficult part was identifying hidden systems that were not directly visible in the movie. This assignment strengthened my ability to view organizations as interconnected systems.
